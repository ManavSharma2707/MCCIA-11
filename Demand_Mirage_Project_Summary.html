The Technical Breakthrough

The "Missing Data" Challenge

If a store doesn't report sales, is it because nobody bought the product (True Zero) or because the store simply didn't upload data (Missing Data)? [cite: 272, 274] Treating missing data as zero artificially deflates demand and triggers dangerous over-ordering [cite: 275].

The Solution

We built a store-level reporting rate threshold to classify missing data points. [cite: 277, 278] Our algorithm identified that while 52.7% were "True Zeros," 42.6% were actually "Missing Data." [cite: 279] Excluding this missing data from training protected the model from systematic underestimation [cite: 280].
